Rules of Engagement

Mission Objective

Your objective is to sink all of your opponent's ships before they can sink all of yours.

You and your opponent each have five ships:

  • Carrier (Five Hits)
  • Battleship (Four Hits)
  • Cruiser (Three Hits)
  • Submarine (Three Hits)
  • Destroyer (Two Hits)

All of your opponent's ships are somewhere on their ocean grid. You will try to hit their ships by selecting coordinates on their grid to fire on. Your opponent will also try to hit your ships by selecting coordinates on your grid. Neither you nor your opponent can see each other's grid, so you must try to guess where each other's ships are. Your display will have two ocean grids – one to display your ship locations and your opponents shots, and one to record your shots at your opponent’s grid.

Before Starting a New Engagement

You and your opponent will each position your 5 ships somewhere on your ocean grids.

  • Ships can only be positioned vertically or horizontally.
  • Diagonal placement is not allowed.
  • No part of a ship may be off the edge of the grid.
  • Ships may not overlap each other.
  • No ships may be placed on another ship.
  • Once the battle begins, ships may not be moved!

Once the Battle Commences

You and your opponent will take turns selecting coordinates to fire upon. Each shot will be noted on your grid as a "hit" or "miss" as appropriate (red for hit, white for miss).

When all of the coordinates that a ship occupies have been hit, the ship will be sunk. You will be informed when you sink an opponent's ship, and they will be informed when they sink one of yours.

Once all of one side's ships have been sunk, the battle is over.
